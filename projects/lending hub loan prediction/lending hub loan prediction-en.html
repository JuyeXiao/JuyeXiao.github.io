The Challenge

16.8% of loans charge off, and the two errors cost wildly different amounts: a missed default loses 4.2 times what a wrongly rejected good loan does. A model tuned only for accuracy answers the wrong question.

The Solution

Three models compete under one leakage-free protocol; LightGBM is selected on lowest out-of-fold cost, and the decision threshold is tuned to 0.16 rather than the default 0.5 to match the cost asymmetry.

The Value

Catches 74.9% of defaults at a total error cost of $2.63M, 26.5% below the $3.58M of approving everyone; losses concentrate in term and the low-grade tail, giving concrete tightening points.

00 Executive Summary

Executive Summary

This analysis of 9,004 completed LendingClub loans answers three questions: how well default can be predicted from application-time data alone, where to set the decision threshold when the two error types cost different amounts, and what actually drives default. The final model is LightGBM on application-time features, tuned for cost — its value is not a leaderboard score but the lowest expected loss.

Four findings

  • Loan term is the single biggest lever. 60-month loans default at 32.4% against 11.3% for 36-month loans, and term tops the SHAP ranking by a wide margin.
  • Default is steeply graded and concentrated. It climbs from 7.4% at grade A to 52.9% at grade G; grades E–G are only 9.4% of loans but account for 22.0% of all defaults.
  • The cost-optimal threshold is 0.16, not 0.5. A missed default costs $9,437 against $2,243 for a wrongly rejected good loan — a 4.2:1 ratio that catches three-quarters of defaults.
  • Interest rate and utilization both raise risk, monotonically. Utilization has no protective middle band, reaching 23.7% default in its top band; higher income pulls risk down.

01 Data & Cost Calibration

Data & Cost Calibration

Each loan carries loan attributes (amount, term, interest rate, installment, grade), borrower attributes (income, employment length, home ownership, state) and credit-file attributes (utilization, account counts, inquiries), plus the Fully Paid or Charged Off label. Quality is high: no duplicate rows and 319 missing cells out of 261,000 (0.1%), confined to emp_length and revol_util.

The dataset also contains repayment-outcome columns (total_pymnt, total_rec_prncp, total_rec_int, out_prncp). These are recorded during or after repayment and are unknown at underwriting — so they are excluded from the features and used only to estimate the cost of each error type.

Cost Calibration · Costs from data, not assumptions

Missed default (false negative) costs the mean unrecovered principal on charged-off loans, funded_amnt − total_rec_prncp, which is $9,437. The full funded amount ($13,663) would overstate the loss by $4,227, because borrowers repay part of the principal before charging off.

Rejected good loan (false positive) costs the mean interest income on fully-paid loans, total_rec_int, which is $2,243.

The resulting 4.2:1 cost ratio is what pulls the optimal threshold well below 0.5.

02 Risk Factors

Risk Factors

Every numeric feature except open_acc separates defaulters from repayers at high significance (p < 0.001), but on 9,000 rows significance is cheap, so effect size is the useful filter. By Cohen's d, interest rate is the one large effect, revolving utilization is moderate, and the rest are small.

Feature Mean (paid) Mean (default) Cohen's d
Interest rate 11.63 14.59 0.73

Annual income 71,110 62,012 −0.19
Inquiries (6 mo) 0.82 1.02 0.19

The categorical breakdown is sharper than any single number. Default climbs monotonically from grade A (7.4%) to grade G (52.9%), and 60-month loans default at nearly three times the 36-month rate.

Counterintuitive · Verified income defaults more

Verified-income loans default more (19.4%) than not-verified ones (14.4%). This counterintuitive result reflects who gets asked to verify rather than any causal effect — verification status should not be read as a safety signal. It is a textbook selection effect.

Banding the continuous drivers shows the relationships are monotonic, not threshold-like. Default rises steadily with revolving utilization, from 10.5% below 20% to 23.7% above 80%, with no dip in the middle, so there is no protective "moderate" band. Income runs the other way, from 20.5% under $40K to 11.1% above $100K.

04 Model Bake-off

Models on Equal Terms

Logistic regression, random forest and LightGBM went through one protocol: outcome columns removed; a 75/25 stratified split; high-cardinality categoricals (addr_state, purpose) encoded with a cross-fitted target encoder that learns from training folds only; SMOTE applied inside the pipeline so synthetic rows never leak into validation; and hyperparameters tuned by 5-fold grid search scoring average precision. Every step that learns from data is fit inside cross-validation, and the threshold is tuned on out-of-fold predictions, so the test set stays an untouched final estimate.

Model Threshold Recall Precision F2 ROC-AUC PR-AUC Total cost
LightGBM 0.16 74.9% 26.9% 0.552 0.733 0.372 $2,627,872
Logistic regression 0.27 77.0% 26.4% 0.557 0.744 0.387 $2,648,817
Random forest 0.19 79.9% 25.4% 0.559 0.733 0.372 $2,715,463

Recall, precision and F2 are computed at each model's cost-optimal threshold; ROC-AUC and PR-AUC are threshold-free. PR-AUC near 0.37 against a 0.168 no-skill baseline is a lift of roughly 2.2×, a realistic level for application-only underwriting.

Model Selection · No model dominates

Logistic regression has the best ranking metrics, random forest the highest recall, LightGBM the lowest cost and best precision. The three finish within 1% of each other on cost, so the choice comes down to the business objective: LightGBM was selected on lowest out-of-fold cost, and logistic regression is a defensible interpretable alternative.

05 Threshold as a Business Decision

Threshold as a Business Decision

The default 0.5 threshold minimizes classification error, but the errors are not equally expensive. With a missed default costing 4.2 times a false alarm, the cost-minimizing cut-off is well below 0.5. The threshold is found on out-of-fold probabilities, subject to catching at least 75% of defaults, and then applied to the test set exactly once.

At threshold 0.16, LightGBM catches 74.9% of test-set defaults at 26.9% precision, for a total error cost of about $2.63M. Approving every applicant, so that every default becomes a loss, would cost $3.58M, so the model lowers expected loss by 26.5%. The low precision is deliberate: at a 4.2:1 cost ratio, flagging some good loans to catch more defaults is the cheaper mistake — flagged loans enter a manual-review queue rather than an automatic decline.

06 Why Loans Default

Why Loans Default: SHAP Attribution

SHAP assigns each feature a signed contribution to each prediction, so the model's reasoning can be read globally and per loan. The attribution runs on the LightGBM model in original feature units, and every direction is checked against the EDA.

Direction Check · Mean signed SHAP misleads

Interest rate raises risk. Its mean signed SHAP is near zero, but that statistic hides the direction. The dependence plot shows a clear positive slope (correlation +0.55 between the rate and its SHAP value), matching the EDA correlation of 0.265 and the large Cohen's d. Mean signed SHAP is the wrong statistic to read direction from; the dependence relationship is the right one.

Utilization has no protective middle zone. Higher utilization pushes risk up throughout, in line with the monotone band chart. Higher income lowers risk (correlation −0.83); a worse grade raises it (+0.91).

07 Actions & Limits

Actions & Limits

Initiative What to do Basis in the analysis
Score at the cost-optimal threshold Run new applications through LightGBM at threshold 0.16; send flagged loans to manual review, not automatic decline. 74.9% of defaults caught, 26.5% lower expected loss than approving all.
Reprice or cap long-term loans Add a risk premium to 60-month loans or restrict them to stronger grades. 60-month default rate 32.4% vs 11.3% for 36-month; term is the top SHAP driver.
Tighten the low-grade tail Apply enhanced review or hard cut-offs at grades E to G. 9.4% of volume but 22.0% of defaults.
Stop treating verification as clearance Do not lower scrutiny because income was verified. Verified loans default more (19.4% vs 14.4%).
Limits

This is a single cross-section of completed loans with no origination dates, so no time-based validation is possible and credit_history_years is a fixed-reference approximation. Interest rate already embeds LendingClub's own risk pricing, so part of its predictive power reflects information the platform encoded at origination rather than an independent borrower signal. The cost figures use portfolio-average FN and FP costs; a production system would price each loan's exposure individually.

Reproducibility

The notebook runs end to end locally (uv run jupyter nbconvert --to notebook --execute). All random seeds are fixed at 42; figures are written to figures/.
